// Research Overview

Why Standard Models Fail

E-commerce order data is full of zeros, variance spikes, and outliers. Classical Poisson regression cannot handle it.

📦 The Problem

Online store orders are count data with overdispersion (variance >> mean) and excess zeros (many time periods record no orders), violating Poisson assumptions and demanding more flexible models.

🎯 Objective

Compare four count regression models — Poisson, NB, ZIP, ZINB — using AIC, BIC, and Log-Likelihood to identify the best fit for e-commerce order forecasting.

✅ Key Finding

ZINB achieved the lowest AIC (1278.66) and highest Log-Likelihood (−636.33), making it the definitive winner. It handles both overdispersion and structural zeros simultaneously.

Data Challenges

  • Overdispersion — Variance far exceeds mean (σ=6.35, μ=11.82), breaking Poisson's equidispersion assumption.
  • Excess Zeros — Many periods have zero orders, far beyond Poisson predictions. ZIP and ZINB use a structural zero component.
  • Right-Skewed Distribution — Counts range from 0 to 34 with a long right tail, making symmetric models inappropriate.
  • Discrete Count Nature — Orders are non-negative integers; continuous regression is fundamentally unsuitable.
  • Surge Periods — Flash sales create extreme high-count spikes that standard models systematically underestimate.
